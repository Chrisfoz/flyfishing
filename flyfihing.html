About Fly Fishing

An art, a sport, a profound connection with nature.

Fly fishing is an angling method using a light-weight lure—an artificial fly—to catch fish. The fly is cast with a fly rod, reel, and specialized weighted line. It demands skill and patience but offers uniquely rewarding experiences in stunning natural settings.

From tranquil streams to expansive lakes, fly fishing immerses you in serene environments. It's more than the catch; it's reading water, understanding insect life, and the rhythmic cast.

Target Species in SA

South Australia offers diverse fly fishing targets. While some perceive it as challenging, the thrill of sight fishing, stalking, and outsmarting your quarry is immensely rewarding. Here are some local favorites:

  • Murray Cod: Our freshwater icon! Tempted with BIG flies (imitating frogs, rats, yabbies) on 8-10wt, sometimes 10-12wt rods. Try northern rivers and dams.
  • Australian Salmon: Torpedo-sized fighters! Use 8-10wt rods with intermediate lines. Popper flies offer thrilling topwater action on beaches like Browns Beach or Morgan Beach.
  • Southern Bluespot Flathead: Great for beginners on flat beaches (Lower Yorke & Eyre Peninsulas). Use 7-8wt outfits with size 1/0 clousers/deceivers on floating lines. Best in autumn/winter.
  • Black Bream: Found in Adelaide’s estuaries. Cunning and shy, requiring stealth, 4-6lb tippets, and 6-7wt rods. Small baitfish or shrimp patterns work well.

More SA Opportunities

  • Mulloway: A true saltwater challenge! Target school fish with 1/0 clousers or big ones with 4/0 garfish flies on 8-10wt outfits. The Coorong (boat) and Onkaparinga River (land) are key spots.
  • Stream Trout (Brown & Rainbow): In Adelaide Hills streams (landowner permission often needed). Use 7-8ft, 3-4wt rods. Small dry flies (size 16-14) in warmer months, nymphs when cooler.
  • Yellowfin Whiting: Fun topwater on flats like Thompsons Beach or Moonta Bay. Use 9ft, 6-8wt outfits with floating lines and popper flies. Crazy Charlies are a good backup.
